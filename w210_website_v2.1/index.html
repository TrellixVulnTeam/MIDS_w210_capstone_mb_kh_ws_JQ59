Background

The Music Industry

The recorded music industry peaked in 1999, followed by a rapid decline of physical CD sales, and total revenues halved by 2009. The good news is that the industry is now experiencing a revival, with year on year growth, driven by paid subscription streaming services.

Oppotunities

Recommendations for Streaming Music

Streaming platforms vie for market share, and one aspect which they compete on is personalized recommendations to help music lovers “discover” songs based on their listening experience. Recommender systems are traditionally content-agnostic. Methods like collaborative filtering need historic user data and may suffer from cold-start problems.

Our Goal

Creative Music Recommendations

Our goal is to create a user-interactive app for song recommendations using deep learning and machine learning techniques with content-based song profiling using embeddings. Instead of recommending songs by their popularity, we find out a fun and interesting path to produce a creative playlist and offer visualizations for user’s interest.

How to process music?

The Magic of Spectrogram

Spectrogram transfers waveform music signals into frequency-intensity 2D images which are ideal data sources for machine learning/deep learning techniques.

Genre Classification

By Convolutional Neural Nets

Pre-trained CNN models were used for genre classifications. Furthermore, we selected the “song embedding” layer of CNN calculated from spectrograms as inputs for the next step: t-SNE clustering.

Playlist Creation

t-SNE 3D Visualization

In order to visualize the 32 dimension song embeddings learned from the CNN, we used T-SNE which stands for t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding to reduce the dimensions to in order to visualize them in an interactive 3D plot. We fine tuned the perplexity hyperparameter and observed the best separation around a value of 40 to 50. This final TSNE coordinates for the embeddings are what we used as the input data for our Neural Notes application.
